Ecological Succession

Primary Succession

Occurs on bare rock. No soil exists. Pioneer species like lichens break rock into soil over long periods.

Secondary Succession

Occurs after a disturbance where soil remains. It is faster because the foundation is already present.

Species Roles

Terms at a Glance

Foundation: Species that engineer the environment (e.g., Corals).
Keystone: Small numbers but huge impact (e.g., Wolves).
Invasive: Non-native species causing harm.

Corals may look more like plants, but they are actually animals. They form coral reefs, making the environment suitable for other organisms. They are “foundation species”.

Species like the Gray Wolf found in small numbers can have a huge impact. We call these “keystone species”. Parrotfish are also keystone species; they feed on macroalgae that would otherwise suffocate corals.

“Invasive” species like Zebra mussels or Japanese knotweed displace native species and damage ecosystems.
