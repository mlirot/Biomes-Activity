3. Succession

Primary

Occurs in areas with no soil (bare rock). Pioneer species like lichens must break down rock first.

Secondary

Occurs after a disturbance where soil remains. Much faster than primary succession.

4. Species Roles

Reading: Ecological Roles

Foundation Species: These species are the "engineers" of an ecosystem. They physically modify the environment to create habitats for others. Example: Coral reefs build the structure that thousands of other species live in.

Keystone Species: These species have a disproportionately large effect on their environment relative to their abundance. If you remove them, the ecosystem often collapses. Example: Wolves in Yellowstone control elk populations, which allows trees to grow and provides homes for birds and beavers.

Invasive Species: These are non-native organisms that cause harm to the ecosystem they are introduced to. They often have no natural predators and outcompete local species for food and space.
