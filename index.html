Foundation Terms

Click a term to see its definition, then try the identification challenge below.

Habitat

The actual physical area where an organism lives. Includes food, water, and shelter.

Niche

The specific role or 'job' an organism has. How it eats, survives, and interacts.

Ecosystem

A community of living organisms interacting with their non-living physical environment.

Biome

A large geographical area with a specific climate and distinct plant/animal life.

Ecological Succession

Primary Succession

[Image of stages of primary succession]

Starts with **Bare Rock** (No Soil). Common after volcanic eruptions or retreating glaciers. Needs **Pioneer Species** (Lichens).

Secondary Succession

[Image of stages of secondary succession]

Starts with **Existing Soil**. Common after forest fires or floods. Much **faster** because nutrients are present.

Which one is faster?

Secondary succession occurs faster because soil and seeds are already there. Nature doesn't have to wait for rocks to break down!

Invasive, Keystone, and Foundation Species

Invasive Species: Non-native organisms that spread rapidly and cause ecological or economic harm. Because they have no natural predators in the new area, they outcompete native species. (Example: Zebra Mussels in the Great Lakes).

Keystone Species: A species that has an unusually large effect on its ecosystem. If they are removed, the entire structure collapses. (Example: Sea Otters control urchin populations to save kelp forests).

Foundation Species: Ecosystem engineers. They physically build the environment that others live in. (Example: Corals that build reefs or Beavers that build dams).
