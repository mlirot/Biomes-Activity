Foundational Terms

Click each card to reveal the definition.

Habitat

The physical place where an organism lives (its "address"). Provides food, water, and shelter.

Niche

The role or "job" of an organism in its environment—how it eats, survives, and reproduces.

Ecosystem

All living (biotic) and non-living (abiotic) factors in an area interacting as a system.

Biome

A large regional area defined by its climate (temperature/precipitation) and vegetation.

Succession: How Ecosystems Change

Primary Succession

Occurs on bare rock. No soil exists (e.g., after a volcano). Pioneer species like lichens break rock into soil over long periods.

Secondary Succession

[Image of stages of secondary succession]

Occurs after a disturbance where soil remains (e.g., forest fire, abandoned farm). It is faster because the foundation is already present.

Species Roles

Invasive Species

Species that are not native to an area and cause harm to the environment or economy. They often outcompete native species because they have no natural predators. Example: Zebra Mussels.

Keystone Species

A species that has a disproportionately large effect on its ecosystem. If removed, the ecosystem might collapse. Example: Sea Otters (who keep sea urchins from eating all the kelp).

Foundation Species

Species that play a major role in creating or maintaining a habitat. They are the "engineers." Example: Corals (who build the reefs that thousands of other species live in).
