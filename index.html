Critical Species Roles

Invasive, Keystone, and Foundation

Invasive Species: Organisms that are not native to an ecosystem and cause harm. They often lack natural predators, allowing them to outcompete local wildlife (e.g., Zebra Mussels).

Keystone Species: A species that holds the ecosystem together. Their impact is much larger than their population size would suggest (e.g., Sea Otters controlling sea urchins).

Foundation Species: Habitat creators. They physically modify the environment to create the base of an ecosystem (e.g., Coral building reefs).
